Accessibility Statement

We are continuously working to improve the accessibility of content on our website.

We are continuously working to improve the accessibility of content on our website. Below you'll find a few recommendations to help make your browsing experience more accessible.

If you have trouble seeing web pages

The US Social Security Administration offers these tips for optimizing your computer and browser to improve your online experience:

  • Use your computer to read web pages out loud
  • Use the keyboard to navigate screens
  • Increase text size
  • Magnify your screen
  • Change background and text colors
  • Make your mouse pointer more visible (Windows only)

If you are looking for alternatives to your mouse and keyboard, speech-recognition software such as Dragon NaturallySpeaking may help you navigate web pages and online services. This software allows the user to move focus around a web page or application screen through voice controls.

If you are deaf or hard of hearing

Closed captioning

Closed captioning provides a transcript for the audio track of a video presentation, synchronized with the video and audio. Captions benefit people who are deaf or hard of hearing, as well as anyone who cannot hear audio due to a noisy environment. Most of the video content on this site is hosted on platforms (such as YouTube) that support captions, which you can turn on and off in the player.

Volume controls

Your computer, tablet, or mobile device has built-in volume-control features, and each video and audio service has its own additional controls. Try adjusting both to optimize your listening experience.

If the recommendations above do not meet your needs, we invite you to contact us at info@appasanimd.com or (424) 432-0452 for assistance.
